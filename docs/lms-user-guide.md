# LMS User Guide

This guide gives a quick overview of how to use the Learning Management System (LMS).

The LMS has three roles:

- `Student`
- `Instructor`
- `Administrator`

## 1. Getting Started

### Register

1. Open the `Register` page.
2. Enter your basic account details.
3. Choose `Student` or `Instructor`.
4. Click `Register`.

Some accounts may require approval or email verification.

### Log in

1. Open the `Login` page.
2. Enter your username or email and password.
3. Click `Login`.

You will be taken to the dashboard for your role.

### Log out

Use the `Logout` button in the sidebar when finished.

## 2. Role Overview

### Student

Students use the LMS to:

- join and open courses
- read lessons
- submit classwork
- take quizzes and exams
- view attendance and grades
- join course discussions

### Instructor

Instructors use the LMS to:

- create and manage courses
- add lessons and activities
- review submissions and grades
- manage meetings and attendance
- track student progress

### Administrator

Administrators use the LMS to:

- manage users and courses
- approve instructors
- view analytics
- update settings
- review logs

## 3. Student Guide

Students can usually access:

- `Dashboard`
- `My Courses`
- `Profile`
- `Settings`

### Open a course

1. Go to `My Courses`.
2. Select a course.
3. Use the course tabs to move through content.

Common course tabs:

- `Stream`
- `Lessons`
- `Classwork`
- `Exams & Quizzes`
- `Meetings`
- `Attendance`
- `Grades`
- `People`
- `Comments`

### What students can do

- `Stream`: View announcements and updates.
- `Lessons`: Open and read learning materials.
- `Classwork`: View tasks and submit work.
- `Exams & Quizzes`: Take assessments and review results if allowed.
- `Meetings`: Check schedules and meeting links.
- `Attendance`: View attendance records.
- `Grades`: Track scores, feedback, and missing work.
- `People`: View course participants.
- `Comments`: Ask questions and join discussions.

### Submit classwork

1. Open the activity.
2. Read the instructions and due date.
3. Upload or enter your work.
4. Submit and confirm the status.

Possible status labels:

- `Pending`
- `Missing`
- `Completed`
- `Graded`

## 4. Instructor Guide

Instructors can usually access:

- `Dashboard`
- `Courses`
- `Analytics`
- `Settings`

### Create a course

1. Open `Courses`.
2. Click `Create Course`.
3. Enter the course details.
4. Save the course.

### Manage a course

Inside a course, instructors can use tabs such as:

- `Stream`
- `Lessons`
- `Classwork`
- `Exams & Quizzes`
- `Meetings`
- `Attendance`
- `Grades`
- `People`
- `Comments`

### Main instructor tasks

- Share the course `Join Code` with students.
- Add lessons and modules.
- Create assignments, projects, quizzes, exams, and materials.
- Review submissions and assign grades.
- Schedule meetings and track attendance.
- Monitor student progress and course analytics.

## 5. Administrator Guide

Administrators can usually access:

- `Overview`
- `All Users`
- `Courses`
- `Pending Instructor Approvals`
- `AI Analytics`
- `Categories`
- `Settings`
- `Logs`

### Main administrator tasks

- `All Users`: Search, filter, and manage accounts.
- `Courses`: Review course records.
- `Pending Instructor Approvals`: Approve instructor accounts.
- `AI Analytics`: Review activity, performance, and risk insights.
- `Categories`: Add, edit, or remove course categories.
- `Settings`: Update platform rules and thresholds.
- `Logs`: Review system activity.

## 6. Quick Tips

- Check the `Stream` regularly for updates.
- Review due dates before submitting work.
- Confirm uploads before leaving the page.
- Check `Grades` often to track progress.
- Use `Comments` for questions or clarification.
- Log out on shared devices.

## 7. Summary

The LMS helps:

- students learn and track progress
- instructors manage teaching and assessment
- administrators manage the platform

Use this guide as a quick daily reference.
